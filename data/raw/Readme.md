# Safety Reports de Cefalosporinas - Base de Datos ADISINSIGHT

Safety Reports son 16 diferentes antibióticos, todos pertenecientes al grupo de las "cefalosporinas":
CEFALOSPORINAS base de datos ADISINSIGHT

La base de datos es ADISINSIGHT (https://adisinsight.springer.com/). La base de datos AdisInsight genera su módulo de “safety reports” a partir del screening manual (por humanos expertos en drug safety y farmacovigilancia) de todas las publicaciones en Journals indexados en MEDLINE y EMBASE que contienen un reporte de caso. Analizan las publicaciones y generan el safety report redactando la narrativa y clasificando los datos y componentes de cada caso.
 
El riesgo principal identificado con estos antibióticos es la neurotoxicidad (convulsiones), pero algunas cefalosporinas confieren más riesgo que otras y también depende del paciente (si tienen insuficiencia renal o no y si tienen antecedentes de transtornos neurológicos).

# USA - FDA Adverse Event Reporting System (FAERS) 
This page displays the number of adverse event reports received by FDA for drugs and therapeutic biologic products by the following Report Types.
• Direct Reports are voluntarily submitted directly to FDA through the MedWatch program by consumers and healthcare professionals.
• Mandatory Reports are submitted by manufacturers and are categorized as:
  i. Expedited reports that contain at least one adverse event that is not currently described in the product labeling and for which the patient outcome is serious, or
  ii. Non-expedited reports that do not meet the criteria for expedited reports, including cases that are reported as Serious and expected, Non-serious and unexpected, and Non-serious and expected.
• BSR Reports are 15-day Biologic Safety Reports which were submitted to FDA as a separate report type until 2005.
• 30-DAY reports are submitted to report product malfunctions of combination products.
• 5-DAY reports are submitted to report serious and unexpected events caused by combination products (products that have both drug and device components) when the event necessitates remedial action to prevent an unreasonable risk of substantial harm to the public health.
